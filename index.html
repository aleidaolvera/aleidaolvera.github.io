I am Aleida, a Computer Science major at Georgetown University with a passion for cybersecurity and technology education.

Hello there! I am Aleida Olvera -- a young Chicana aspiring to become a cybersecurity professional and leader in technology education.

On campus, I am a Student Web Developer Intern for the School of Foreign Service, Microsoft Student Partner, and Georgetown Community Scholar. I hope to partner with a company during the Summer of 2017 that will allow me to continue to explore my passion for any form of computer science.

In the past, I have held office as the 2015 - 2016 Texas Technology Student Association Vice President, founded my high school’s first Girls Who Code Club. Along with this, I traveled to the White House as an Entertainment Software Association LOFT Innovation Fellow and the Intel International Science and Engineering Fair to present SmartGuard: an innovative safety application to tackle my community’s safety issues. I have been blessed to represent the National Center for Women and Information Technology as a Technolochica and a 2015 National Aspirations in Computing Awardee, as well the Hispanic Heritage Foundation as an ESA Loft Innovation Fellow and National Hispanic Heritage Youth Awardee for Innovation and Technology.
