Designing your website

Tips

  1. If your audience cannot tell what your website is about in less than 4 seconds your website needs to be changed. People come looking for information. If they cannot figure out what your website is about in a few seconds: you and your website have not done its job.

  2. Keep your layout and color scheme consistent throughout your website. If your color scheme changes with every page, or if your layout changes your audience will be confused with every page.

  3. Keep your navigation links and header the same throughtout all the pages and your audience will never be lost!

  4. AVOID AT ALL COSTS: Avoid W3Schools, their information may seem reliable, but their tutorials have inconsistencies thoroughtout the entire website. You are better off learning from the our sources listed down below.

Other Awesome HTML & CSS Tutorial Websites (Our Sources)

  • W3C HTML & CSS - Our most acreditted priary source comes straight from the creaters themselves! W3 is the organization that regulates the web coding standards.

  • The MDN (Mozilla's Developer Network) - This website takes over at intermediate CSS and covers JavaScript better than anyone! It comes straight from Mozilla, the developers of the popular web browser Firefox!

  • WebPlatform.org - This source is an excellent website to learn HTML & CSS as well as many other essential tools to web development.

  • Codecademy - This website provides interactive tutorials, for all things code! From HTML & CSS to PHP & Ruby (these last two are other languages used in Web Development.)
